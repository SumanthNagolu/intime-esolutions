## Questions

### Question 1

**Question Text:**
What does FNOL stand for in the context of insurance claims?

**Options:**
A) Final Notice of Liability
B) First Notice of Loss
C) Formal Notice of Litigation
D) Financial Notice of Loss

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation (optional):**
FNOL (First Notice of Loss) is the initial report made to an insurance company following loss, theft, or damage of an insured asset. It's the first step in the claims process.

---

### Question 2

**Question Text:**
Which ClaimCenter component is responsible for automatically routing claims to appropriate adjusters?

**Options:**
A) Workflow Manager
B) Assignment Engine
C) Activity Service
D) Claim Service

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation (optional):**
The Assignment Engine uses configurable rules to automatically assign claims to adjusters based on factors like workload, expertise, geography, and claim complexity.

---

### Question 3

**Question Text:**
In ClaimCenter, what is the primary purpose of Exposures?

**Options:**
A) To track claim expenses
B) To represent potential financial liability for specific coverages
C) To manage document attachments
D) To schedule claim activities

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation (optional):**
Exposures represent the insurance company's potential financial liability under a specific coverage. Each claim can have multiple exposures (e.g., bodily injury, property damage).

---

### Question 4

**Question Text:**
Which of the following is NOT a standard claim status in ClaimCenter?

**Options:**
A) Open
B) Closed
C) Suspended
D) Approved

**Correct Answer:** D

**Explanation (optional):**
"Approved" is not a standard claim status. Standard statuses include Open, Closed, and variations like Draft. "Approved" might apply to specific activities or payments, but not the overall claim status.

---

### Question 5

**Question Text:**
What is the role of Reserves in ClaimCenter?

**Options:**
A) To store backup copies of claim data
B) To estimate and track the expected payout amount for a claim
C) To reserve adjuster time for future work
D) To maintain audit logs

**Correct Answer:** B

**Explanation (optional):**
Reserves are financial estimates set aside to cover expected claim payouts. They help insurance companies maintain appropriate financial reserves and are adjusted throughout the claim lifecycle as more information becomes available.
